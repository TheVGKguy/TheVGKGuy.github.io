Legoman's projects

If there is one thing Legoman likes to do on Scratch, is creating projects for all of Scratch to see. Let's look at some of Legoman's projects.

Image and Link Title and Description

Any Key?


"Any Key?" was Legoman's first project. He first got the idea from an episode of "The Simpsons" and thought it would be funny to make something similar to that and shareit with his friends. Despite some uneeded people telling Legoman how to pronounce control, Legoman's friends liked the project very much. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

May the Forks be With You!


"May the Forks be With You" was a combination of the Star Wars day May the Fourth be With You and Cinco de Mayo. Legoman was originally going to create a May the Fourth project and a Cinco de Mayo project, but time was unkind to Legoman, so Legoman combined the two animations. No one was expecting Legoman to make this combination, but everyone liked the idea.To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Scratch Week: AWOS


"Scratch Week" is a famous week on Scratch where the people at MIT first created Scratch. In order to celebrate this week, Legoman created an animation that celebrates Scratch Cat and Pico, Scratch's other character. Legoman decided to create the animation with only Scratch characters (that's where the AWOS comes in) after seeing Dinoboi_Animations create a similar thing. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Legoman and Little Lego


Legoman wanted to create a new character that can be Legoman's sidekick when Legoman's fighting crime. So, Legoman created a Lego minifigure and called him Little Lego. Little Lego was introduced into the world of Legoman when he was waking Legoman up from a nice sleep. Poor Legoman. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Little Lego's (Failed) Adventure


Due to the fact that Little Lego wants some action in his life and is tired of waking Legoman up, Little Lego is going on an adventure by himself. This project shows people how Little Lego's adventure goes. Poor Little Lego. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

WazzoTV Before/After griffpatch


"WazzoTV Before/After griffpatch" was the first entry for Coolbe22's 3K animation contest. Legoman got the idea for this animation when he saw the fact that WazzoTV has been on Scratch longer than griffpatch has. However, griffpatch has more followers than WazzoTV. Despite not winning anything in the contest, Legoman got the ability to make people laugh with his semi-lip-syncing animation. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Legoman's Day Off


Originally, "Legoman's Day Off" was Legoman's first animation, but was remastered because Legoman_Animator's first draft of Legoman wasn't very crowd-pleasing. The first draft of Legoman had a yellow shirt with a red Lego-piece in the middle. Somehow, people were disgusted by this version. So, Legoman_Animator edited Legoman (once) until the crowd was pleased with his apperence. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Spam Be Like...


"Spam Be Like..." was an entry for PaddlerGames's 7k Animation Contest. It was also the first project to introduce Legoman's new OC that was designed by EVERYTHINGWASTAKEN12. Legoman wanted a new OC (original character) because he wanted to lip-sync his animations, and his old OC wasn't making that easy. And despite Legoman's lost in the contest, many people could relate to the animation. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Legoman Showcase


"Legoman Showcase" was the second entry for PaddlerGames's 7k Animation Contest. Since Legoman changed his OC, he thought it was right to give Legoman's old OC a memorial. So, Legoman created a project that included all of the animations that Legoman's old OC starred in. From "Any Key?" to "Legoman's Day Off." To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Getting Requests (collab with @hello-rhino)


"Getting Requests" is a collab between Legoman_Animator and hello-rhino. Legoman got the idea when he saw how many requests for intros and outros hello-rhino was getting while Legoman wasn't getting any requests for pfps (profile pictures). So, Legoman decided to have a little fun with this idea. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

400! (collab with @TheComputerIsCoool)


"400!" was a collab between Legoman_Animator and TheComputerIsCool. Both Legoman and Computer got 400 at the same time (Computer had 400 quicker, but who was keeping track?), as a celebration for 400, both Legoman and Computer made a collab of how they felt when they reached 400. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

It took a few days for NotYourAverageAnime to see the animation, but when he did see, he was happy that Legoman took the time to create something just for him. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

And as the first guy Legoman asked, marshmello3_4 was happy to accept the invitation. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

And some people believed the other guy until Legoman said something. But during the time of the legoman-animater, many people changed their credits to Legoman. And that's how Legoman got the idea of an animation. To see the project, click on the image to the left.

Block Combat||1st remix
