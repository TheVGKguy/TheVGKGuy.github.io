Scratch's Downfall

Created on June 1, 2022

During his one year on Scratch, Legoman had seen changes to Scratch. Some good, some bad. For example, Legoman noticed that the studios got an update. The design was cool, but the new update also got more strict on the rules. Like having a 25 comment limit on a comment thread before automatically closing the thread, only allowing 40 people to be a manager, and only the studio's creator can change the description of the studio. These changes caused many people to be furious and a few people to leave Scratch. Another change Legoman has seen is that the filterbots have gotten more sensitive and are now muting people for basically everything. One of Legoman's friends tried to talk in Morse Code, and she got muted for 20 minutes. "I am tired of these filterbots," Legoman said, "they are too strict." And the final change Legoman saw before leaving Scratch was that Scratch was going to get rid of Turbowarp's (a division of Scratch) ability to see unshared projects. Almost everyone was mad at this update because that meant people can't collab, see the first Scratch project, or see a project that was wrongfully taken down. "The Scratch Team is limiting our freedom with these updates," spoke Legoman, "None of the Scratchers will wanna use Scratch much longer." Legoman's prediction became correct, and thousands of Scratchers left Scratch for a different social media app that has less rules, like Youtube. In this reporter's opinion, I think everyone should quit using Scratch so the Scratch Team can learn their lesson.
